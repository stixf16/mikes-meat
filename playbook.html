01

The Two-Engine StrategyHow eyeballs turn into orders

Two engines drive traffic to the exact same products. Both point at the TikTok Shop and Shopify checkout.

Engine A

People In The Merch

Anyone who wears the gear and posts it — friends, gym crew, customers, local creators — is free reach. Each post borrows that person’s audience and points them at checkout. Hand every wearer a ready-made hook (below) so posting is effortless, and the more who post, the bigger the launch.

Engine B

Mike’s Comedy Reels

Mike posts short, funny AI-made videos on his own TikTok 5–7×/week. The centaur mascot, gym humor, and patriotism keep the algorithm fed and give people a reason to follow him, not just buy once.

The product line online = shirts, hats, aprons, stickers with the centaur logo. That’s it for launch. Add SKUs later once fulfillment is smooth.
02

Stand Up TikTok ShopPrimary storefront — where the influencer sends people

  1. Get a TikTok Business account
    In the TikTok app: Profile → Settings → Account → switch to a Business account. Use the Mike’s Meat handle you’ll post from.
  2. Apply at TikTok Shop Seller Center
    Go to seller-us.tiktok.com and register as a seller. You’ll pick “US Seller.”
  3. Verify the business
    Upload ID and business info (EIN or sole-prop details). Approval is usually a day or two. This is the step that gates everything — start it early.
  4. Connect a payout bank account
    TikTok collects the customer’s payment and deposits your cut to the linked bank account on a schedule. No separate Stripe needed for TikTok Shop itself.
  5. List the products
    Add each item: clean photos (on a body + flat-lay), title, description in the brand voice, price, sizes, and quantity on hand. Do this for shirts, hats, aprons, stickers.
  6. Set shipping templates
    Enter package weight + size per item so TikTok can quote the buyer an estimate by ZIP at checkout. (Phase 04 shows how to get those real numbers.)
  7. Go live & tag products in content
    Flip the shop on. Then products can be tagged in Mike’s videos and the influencer’s post so viewers tap straight to checkout.
Fulfillment reality: TikTok is the storefront + payment. Mike ships every order himself from the garage. TikTok does not touch, store, or ship the product.
03

Stand Up ShopifyThe home base everyone off-TikTok can find

TikTok reaches the TikTok crowd. Shopify catches everyone else — Google searches, shared links, QR codes on the jerky bags at the gym.

  1. Sign up & pick a plan
    Start the trial at shopify.com. The Basic plan (roughly $30-ish/mo, less if paid annually) is plenty to launch.
  2. Claim the store name / domain
    You start on a free name.myshopify.com address, then buy a custom domain (e.g. mikesmeat.com) inside Shopify — a few bucks a month.
  3. Choose & brand a theme
    Pick a clean free theme, drop in the centaur logo, and set the scarlet/gray palette. Claude Code can customize the theme beyond the defaults for a sharper, on-brand look — Stix can build anything with Claude Code.
  4. Turn on Shopify Payments
    Enable Shopify Payments (or connect Stripe/PayPal). This is how cards get charged at checkout.
  5. Add the same products
    Re-use the exact photos, copy, prices, and inventory counts from TikTok so both stores match.
  6. Set shipping rates by weight & zone
    Enter the real rates from Phase 04 so checkout charges the right amount. Offer flat-rate tiers to keep it simple (e.g. 1 item / 2–3 items / 4+).
  7. Launch & link everywhere
    Remove the password page, then put the link in Mike’s TikTok bio, on stickers, and on a QR code by the gym jerky stand.
Out of stock? On both platforms you just set the item to 0 quantity / unavailable and it grays out. Flip it back on when Mike restocks. No penalty.
04

Shipping & FulfillmentGet the real numbers so you don’t lose money per box

  1. Open a business shipping account
    Sign up for USPS business shipping and/or a UPS business account. Business/commercial rates beat walk-in counter prices and let you print labels at home.
  2. Weigh & measure a packed box
    Put 1 shirt in a real mailer and weigh it. Then 2 items, then 3. Note the box dimensions too — carriers price on weight and size.
  3. Price 3 zones × 3 weights
    Use the carrier calculator with a near ZIP (NV), a mid ZIP (TX), and a far ZIP (NY/FL) at each weight. That grid shows your true cost spread.
  4. Set flat-rate tiers with a small buffer
    Pick simple buyer-facing prices (1 item / 2–3 / 4+) that cover the worst zone plus a couple dollars of padding, tape, and mailer cost. Enter those on both stores.
  5. Print label → pack → drop off
    Order comes in → print the label from the carrier account → box it in the garage → hand off or schedule pickup. Consider USPS flat-rate boxes for heavier multi-item orders.
Tip: TikTok Shop and Shopify both have shipping-label tools built in that can pull discounted rates — worth comparing against the raw USPS/UPS business rate once you’re running volume.
05

Social Media PlaybookFunny wins. Consistency wins more.

Cadence

Post 5–7× a week on Mike’s TikTok. On TikTok, consistency beats perfect timing — the algorithm rewards volume + watch-time, not the clock. Batch-film several at once, then drip them out daily.

The Vibe

Absurdist and self-aware, like those over-the-top Dr. Squatch / Sasquatch soap ads — but centered on a shirtless bald centaur waving a flag. Gym-bro humor + patriotism + “only in America” energy. Don’t sell hard; make people laugh and the merch sells itself.

Hooks for Anyone in the Merch

Hand these to every person who wears the gear so posting takes zero thought. Ask them to tag the account and use the same hashtag every time so the posts stack up.

“Fit check” in the Mike’s Meat tee Grilling / gym session in the apron “Guess what this centaur is selling” Slap the sticker somewhere wild, film it “Only in America” wearing the hat Hold up the jerky, rep the shirt Duet / stitch Mike’s latest video in the merch Tag @mikesmeat + same hashtag every post

Keep it dead simple: wear it, tag it, be funny. No follower count required — ten friends posting beats waiting on one big account.

Mike’s HomeworkAnswer these — they unlock pricing & go-live

Tap into each box and type your answer. Hit Save and it stays on your phone. These four numbers are what turn this plan into live listings.

1 · Inventory on hand

How many of each do you physically have right now?

2 · Restock speed

When you sell out, how fast can you get more — and what’s the minimum order / cost from your supplier?

3 · Your prices

What will each item sell for? (Cost + margin. Don’t forget you also collect shipping separately.)

4 · Shipping math

Packed weight + box size for 1 item, and the price you got for a near / mid / far ZIP.

Bonus · Launch signals

Is the influencer locked in with a date? Any budget at all? Anything else you want on the plan?
